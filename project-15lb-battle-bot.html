15lb Combat Robot

A 15lb combat robot designed and built from scratch by a 6-person team for SBB competition (Sacramento Battle Bots) 2025.

Quick video of Master Oogway going through safety checks.

Project Overview

As a member of Combat Robotics at Berkeley, I collaborated with a team of six to design, CAD, and build a 15lb metal combat robot. The project was completed over a 4-month timeline with a budget of $1500.

My primary contributions were in mechanical design and manufacturing, specifically engineering the drivetrain, invertibility, and fabricating custom axles. I also assisted in component selection for the Bill of Materials (BOM) and CADing the frame.

  • Duration: 4 Months (Approx. Dec 2024 - April 2025)
  • Team Size: 6 Students
  • Budget: $1500
  • My Role: Mechanical Designer (Drivetrain), Machinist, Systems Integration
  • Tools & Tech: Fusion, Manual Lathe, Manual Mill, Drill Press, GD&T, 3D Printing

Mechanical Design & CAD

My lead design role was engineering the robot's drivetrain. A key requirement was invertibility, meaning the robot had to be fully functional even when flipped upside-down. I designed the system in CAD to ensure the wheels would maintain contact with the ground in either orientation, protecting the chassis while remaining mobile.

I also assisted in CADing the main structural frame and spearheaded the creation of the comprehensive BOM, streamlining our acquisition of all mechanical and electrical components.

CAD render of Master Oogway.

Manufacturing & Assembly

I took a hands-on role in manufacturing several critical components. My main responsibility was fabricating the custom weapon and drive axles.

Using the manual lathe and tapping tools, I cut steel stock to precise lengths, drilled holes, and tapped them. Then, using a CNC mill and tapping tools, I created tapped threads for our weapon hub. Simillary, I also tapped the wheels and Aluminum drive axles.

Assembled Master Oogway

Results & Conclusion

Our team successfully completed the robot within the budget and timeline, and it passed all safety and functionality checks for competition. The invertible drivetrain I designed performed exceptionally well, allowing the bot to remain a threat even after being flipped.
